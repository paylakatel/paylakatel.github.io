Earth Images

I recently figured out how to make true and false color images in GIMP using Landsat data and I've had a lot of fun creating my own images of different places.

Provincetown, MA. True color composite (432). Landsat 8 OLI; October 8, 2015.
The very tip of Cape Cod, MA can be seen in this image in true color. The red, green, and blue bands are used to show what the area looks like in the visible spectral range.


Venice, Italy. False color composite (564). Landsat 8 OLI; April 19, 2015.
This band combination uses the NIR, SWIR, and Red bands. Vegetation shows as shades of browns with darker colors indicating a higher soil moisture content, seen in the upper right and lower left corners of the image. Urban areas appear grey, seen on the island of Venice proper in the center of the image.


West of Anchorage, Alaska. False color composite (654). Landsat 8 OLI; August 14, 2014.
This band combination uses the SWIR, NIR, and Red bands. Vegetation is highlighted in this combination and appears bright green.


Southern region of Lake Volta, Ghana. False color composite (432). Landsat 8 OLI; January 4, 2015.
This band combination is the most common false color composite. It uses the NIR, Red, and Green bands. Vegetation appears shades of bright red and pink in images like this and urban areas are blue/gray.


Northern part of Lake Volta, Ghana. False color composite (752). Landsat 8 OLI; January 4, 2015.
This band combination uses the SWIR, NIR, and Blue bands. Vegetation appears bright green in these composites and bare soil appears yellow/brown.
